Group Reflection

A short collective reflection and individual notes on what worked, what needs improvement and next steps.

Group overview

We collaborated across roles to design interactive, learner-centred lesson plans and resources. Below are a concise group reflection and each member's individual notes.

"Working together strengthened both our design choices and our ability to use digital tools for engaging lessons."

Group Reflection (100 words)

Our group collaborated effectively by combining diverse ideas and skills. We learned how to integrate digital tools to make English learning interactive and fun. The project improved our understanding of technology-enhanced teaching and strengthened our teamwork abilities. We will improve planning time and technical rehearsals next time to avoid last-minute issues and better support learners who need extra vocabulary scaffolding.

Individual Reflections

Nsovo Masingi — Project Lead & Website developer

What went well: The group effectively shared resources and ideas, enhancing the overall project quality.

Needs improvement: Time management during discussions could be better.

Next step: Implement a timed agenda for future meetings to stay on track.

Siniko Ntlamba — Lesson Developer

What went well: Learners enjoyed the writing assignment and showed good engagement with the reading.

Needs improvement: Some learners required more vocabulary assistance.

Next step: Introduce a speaking and listening exercise on the same subject for the following lesson (e.g., group discussion about favourite healthy meals).

Hitekani Mafuyeka — Lesson plan developer and administrator

What went well: The use of digital tools facilitated collaboration and made the learning process more engaging.

Needs improvement: Some technical issues arose during the presentation.

Next step: Conduct a tech check before future presentations to avoid disruptions.
